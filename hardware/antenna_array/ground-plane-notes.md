# Ground Plane Notes

## Purpose
The ground plane and mounting surface affect repeatability, coupling, and physical durability. This note records the working assumptions for prototype 1.

## Current assumptions
- Use a rigid conductive plate, likely aluminum.
- Keep the surface flat enough that antenna tilt is negligible.
- Avoid flexible backing materials for the first prototype.
- Mark antenna positions directly on the plate or on a fixed template.

## Mechanical considerations
- Corners should not present obvious handling hazards.
- The plate should support clean cable routing and strain relief.
- The assembly should be portable without the antenna locations shifting.

## Open items
- final plate thickness
- exact dimensions
- whether a secondary support frame is required
- whether field portability requires edge protection or enclosure integration
